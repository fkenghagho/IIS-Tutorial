Analysis of 5-card poker hand probabilities using combinatorial methods.

1. Number of Atomic Events (Total Combinations)

The number of different combinations of five cards that can be drawn from a standard 52-card deck is given by the binomial coefficient (or "52 choose 5"), which represents the total number of possible 5-card hands. This is the size of the joint probability distribution space.

Formula for Combinations:

$$\\ \text{Total Hands} = \binom{n}{k} = \binom{52}{5} = \frac{52!}{5!(52-5)!} = \frac{52 \times 51 \times 50 \times 49 \times 48}{5 \times 4 \times 3 \times 2 \times 1}$$

Result:

$$2,598,960 \quad \text{atomic events}$$

There are 2,598,960 unique 5-card hands possible.

2. Probability of a Single Atomic Event

Since all hands are equally likely (assuming an honest dealer), the probability of any single, specific 5-card hand (an atomic event) is the reciprocal of the total number of hands.

Calculation:

$$\\ P(\text{Atomic Event}) = \frac{1}{\text{Total Hands}} = \frac{1}{2,598,960}$$

Approximate Value:

$$\approx 0.0000003848$$

3. Probability of a Royal Straight Flush (A, K, Q, J, 10 of same suit)

A Royal Straight Flush is defined by five specific ranks (A, K, Q, J, 10) and one specific suit.

  • There are 4 suits (Spades, Hearts, Diamonds, Clubs).
  • In each suit, there is only 1 possible Royal Straight Flush (e.g., A♠, K♠, Q♠, J♠, 10♠).
  • Total number of Royal Straight Flushes = 4.

Calculation:

$$\\ P(\text{Royal Straight Flush}) = \frac{\text{Number of Royal Flushes}}{\text{Total Hands}} = \frac{4}{2,598,960}$$

Result:

$$\approx 0.000001539 \quad \text{or} \quad 1 \text{ in } 649,740$$

4. Probability of Four Equal Cards (Four of a Kind)

A Four of a Kind hand consists of four cards of the same rank (e.g., four Queens) and one fifth card (the kicker).

  • Step 1: Choose the rank for the four equal cards. There are $\binom{13}{1} = 13$ possible ranks (2 through A).
  • Step 2: Choose the four cards of that rank. Since all four cards of that rank are required (e.g., all four Queens), there is only $\binom{4}{4} = 1$ way to choose them.
  • Step 3: Choose the kicker (the fifth card). The kicker can be any of the remaining $52 - 4 = 48$ cards.

Total number of Four of a Kind hands:

$$\\ \text{Four of a Kind Hands} = \binom{13}{1} \times \binom{4}{4} \times \binom{48}{1} = 13 \times 1 \times 48 = 624$$

Probability Calculation:

$$\\ P(\text{Four of a Kind}) = \frac{\text{Number of Four of a Kind Hands}}{\text{Total Hands}} = \frac{624}{2,598,960}$$

Result:

$$\approx 0.0002401 \quad \text{or} \quad 1 \text{ in } 4,165$$
