URDF Design Challenge

The Domestic Assistant Robot

Apply modularity and parameterization to design a functional home robot.

The Challenge: Dishwasher Unloader

Your task is to design a complete robot model in URDF (using Xacro for modularity) that is capable of performing a complex domestic task: emptying a dishwasher and placing the clean items into a nearby cabinet.

You must build upon the concepts of composition (creating separate files for the base, arm, and gripper) and parameterization (using ${param_name} variables) to make your design highly reusable and configurable.

Environment Specifications (Dishwasher & Cabinet)

Dishwasher

  • Form: Hollow cubic volume (0.6m W x 0.6m D x 0.85m H).
  • Location: Directly placed on the floor.
  • Door: Horizontal axis opening (flips down).
  • Dishes (Initial State): Bowls and plates are ranged vertically on a pull-out rack.
  • Handle: Small physical handle present for opening/closing.

Cabinet

  • Form: Hollow cubic volume (0.8m W x 0.4m D x 1.0m H), placed on a countertop (0.7m H).
  • Location: 10.0 meter away from the dishwasher, requiring the robot to maneuver.
  • Door: Vertical axis opening (swings open to the side).
  • Dishes (Final State): Bowls and plates must be ranged horizontally on shelves.
  • Handle: Small physical handle present for opening/closing.

These specifications directly influence the required robot arm reach, the degrees of freedom (DOF), and the necessary gripper capabilities (e.g., handling thin plates vertically vs. placing them horizontally).

Key Design Considerations

1. The Mobile Base

  • Does your robot need to move?
  • What type of wheels are necessary (Omni, Diff-Drive)?
  • What parameters define its size and stability (e.g., ${base_width}, ${wheel_radius})?

2. The Manipulator (Arm)

  • How many degrees of freedom (DOF) are required to reach both the bottom of the dishwasher and the cabinet shelves?
  • Which joints will you use (e.g., revolute, prismatic)?
  • What parameters control its reach and strength (e.g., ${link_length_1}, ${max_effort})?

3. The End-Effector (Gripper)

  • What kind of gripper can handle plates, cups, and cutlery?
  • Should the gripper be an independent component (its own file) included in the arm?
  • What parameters govern its opening size (e.g., ${gripper_max_span})?

4. Modularity Structure

  • Define separate files: base.xacro, arm.xacro, and gripper.xacro.
  • Create a top-level file, dishwasher_robot.xacro, to include and connect the components.

Prepare Your Defense

You must be ready to defend your design by explaining:

  • Why you chose a specific number of DOF for the arm.
  • How your choice of parameters allows for future modifications without rewriting component files.
  • The connection strategy (joints and origins) between the base, arm, and gripper.
